Data Collected

Data Collected Prior to Research

Based on the data collected through observation, there are some clear discoveries common across multiple participants. Before the study started, we gathered general information on how participants used their Microsoft Windows computers.

Appendix A.

As seen in Appendix A, we can see that all participants used their computers for general tasks such as internet browsing. One participant used their computer for tertiary studies. Another participant used their computer for work, whereas two out of the three users used their computer for recreational activities such as gaming.

Appendix B.

All participants had extensive experience using Microsoft Windows, though two out of three participants had limited MacOS experience (See Appendix B.). All participants had not used Linux or another operating system before, excluding mobile operating systems.

Observation Stage

The observation stage took approximately 5-10 minutes and allowed participants to experiment with the operating system and become acquainted. Most of the participants had similar reactions to the operating system. Most felt that at face value, Ubuntu seemed similar to Windows. Two out of the three users explored the operating systems settings, preinstalled applications and browsing. The participant with the least technical operating system knowledge explored the preinstalled applications and navigated the many categories Linux uses to organise its file structure.
