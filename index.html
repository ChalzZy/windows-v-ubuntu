Introduction

Ubuntu is one of the dozens of popular operating systems available on the market. Being a distribution of Linux, Ubuntu is an open-source operating system known for its improved privacy and security compared to Microsoft’s Windows. Ubuntu currently hosts over twenty-five million daily users. On the other hand, Windows is the largest operating system globally, dominating the market share with 1.3 billion devices running Windows 10 and hosting over 91% of the world's desktop computers (Thomas, 2007). This study will investigate individuals with long-term Windows usage and their experience using the Ubuntu operating system for the first time, focusing on Ubuntu’s usability as a fully-fledged graphical operating system designed for daily use to gather an understanding of how Windows and alternatives compare. The reason behind this research topic is based on our prior experience using Ubuntu as computer science students, where we found Ubuntu to be a potentially viable alternative and became interested in its usability as a primary operating system for the general population.

Background information on study

Due to Ubuntu being a popular operating system in commercial environments, such as use for servers, this research's primary interest is based on the general population's usability of Ubuntu as a personal, fully-fledged graphical operating system, similar to Windows. With so many operating systems available, many of which are open source and free, interest was also taken to the different types available to consumers and why Microsoft Windows dominates the market share by such a considerable margin. Ubuntu stood out as a clear open-source alternative to Windows, known for its ease of use and welcoming appearance (Helmke et al., 2013), which led to Ubuntu becoming the primary basis for comparison, with further interest in long term Windows users opinions and transferability of skills when working with Ubuntu for the first time.
