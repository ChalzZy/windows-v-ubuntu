Conclusion

In conclusion, the study successfully identified the initial usability of the Ubuntu operating system by long term Windows users. All participants had overall positive initial impressions when using Ubuntu and contributed their ease of use to prior experience based on their Windows usage. All participants experienced Ubuntu as a fully-fledged graphical operating system compared to its traditional commercial form and agreed that Ubuntu was a viable alternative with enough features to become an alternative to Windows.

The main findings conclude that participants could successfully complete all assigned tasks, with one task proving to be slightly more complicated. This includes changing the resolution, installing Google Chrome and changing the background. Two of three participants experienced problems with the installing Google Chrome step, both of which were related to a difference in Ubuntuâ€™s installation process compared to Windows. The ease of changing the resolution and background can be linked to the fact that these two tasks are very similar to the Windows equivalent while installing an application such as Google Chrome differs.

These findings prove further prove that prior Windows experience played a crucial role in the use of Ubuntu.

In order to further this research, we would first increase the number of participants to understand users' experiences more and gain further insights and similarities between participants, as well and introducing more demographic variants to investigate how particular demographics display Ubuntu usability skills differently.
